Marmots are large ground squirrels that live in mountainous areas, and they spend most of their lives in burrows, which they dig themselves to provide shelter from predators and harsh weather conditions.
Although marmots are generally shy and avoid human contact, they are highly social animals within their colonies, often communicating with each other through a series of whistles and warning calls to alert the group of potential danger.
During the warmer months, marmots forage for grasses, berries, and flowers to build up fat reserves, because they hibernate for nearly eight months in a deep sleep that helps them survive the long, cold winters when food is scarce.
